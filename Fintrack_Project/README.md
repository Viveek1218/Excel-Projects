# FinTrack Financial Analytics Dashboard

## Project Summary
This project presents an interactive financial analytics dashboard built in Microsoft Excel to evaluate business performance across multiple dimensions. It enables analysis of key metrics such as sales, profit, units sold, and profit margin, while providing insights into trends, discount strategies, and performance drivers.

The objective is to transform raw financial data into actionable insights that support data-driven decision-making.

---

## Objectives
The dashboard is designed to address the following business questions:

- Analyze overall sales and profitability performance  
- Identify top-performing segments, products, and regions  
- Examine sales trends over time, including Year-over-Year (YoY) growth  
- Evaluate the impact of discounts on sales and profitability  
- Detect seasonal patterns to support planning and forecasting  

---

## Dataset Overview
The dataset contains multi-year financial and sales data with the following structure:

### Dimensions
- Country  
- Segment  
- Product  
- Discount Band  

### Key Metrics
- Sales (Net)  
- Profit  
- Units Sold  
- Discounts  
- Profit Margin (%)  

### Time Coverage
- Monthly data aggregated into quarterly and yearly views  

The dataset was cleaned and structured for analysis using pivot tables and dynamic filtering.

---

## Dashboard Features

### KPI Overview
- Net Sales  
- Total Profit  
- Units Sold  
- Profit Margin (%) with conditional indicators  

### Performance Drivers
- Profit analysis by Segment  
- Profit analysis by Product  

### Trend Analysis
- Sales trends over time  
- Year-over-Year comparison of Sales and Profit  

### Discount Impact Analysis
- Sales and Profit by Discount Band  
- Highlights trade-offs between revenue growth and profitability  

### Interactive Filtering
- Slicers: Country, Segment, Product  
- Timeline filter for time-based analysis  

---

## Key Insights
- A small number of segments and products contribute significantly to overall profit  
- Sales and profit show clear trends over time with periods of growth and fluctuation  
- Discount strategies have a direct impact on both revenue and profitability  
- Moderate discounts tend to balance sales growth and profit, while excessive discounts reduce margins  
- Seasonal patterns indicate opportunities for improved planning and forecasting  

---

## Business Recommendations
- Optimize discount levels to balance sales growth and profitability  
- Focus on high-performing segments and products  
- Use historical trends and seasonality for demand forecasting and planning  
- Leverage the dashboard for continuous, data-driven performance monitoring
